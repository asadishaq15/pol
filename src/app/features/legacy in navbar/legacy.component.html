PEARLS OF MY LYFE are the things that I have cherished the most. There are many experiences that bring us joy and we wish to share with family. Things like the old neighborhood, spending time with grandparents, old friends, old hangout places, the way things used to be or older family members who instilled value in you.

These are all pearls that each one of us have experienced and know carry with us. They should not be tossed away or forgotten upon our graduation into eternity.

What if POML existed in the past? You could click a place in this website and see an old family member talking to you about the things they cherished the most and want to leave the memory behind for you to have (smile); or what if they left behind some wisdom that could keep you out of trouble and on a good path in life. That is how it is supposed to be, family passing down traditions, wisdom, knowledge and many other things.

Sometimes we don’t have the words when we are in front of loved ones. Some relationships are estranged and it’s hard to communicate. You still care about them and have value to give them. Now, you can take your time, think about what this life and your loved ones have meant to you; and leave it all in your PEARLS OF MY LYFE account.

If you don’t know where to start and need help. Visit the “moments to share” section to spark ideas.

Moments to Share Suggestion

Below are some examples of memories you can share when leaving the details of the life your living. Remember…Your whole life is your legacy. The experiences, laughter, lessons, etc. Let’s get started!

  • Favorite memories about your friends (childhood, adulthood)
  • Family pictures. Explain who the people are in the picture; where and when that picture was taken; who took the picture; what led up to the event that day, etc.
  • Explain some of the best foods you’ve ever tasted; where you tasted; who made it; who makes it best in your opinion; why you are partial to this/these foods.
  • List some of your favorite places to visit, and why.
  • Reminisce about some of your favorite childhood games; how to play them; why you enjoyed them.
  • Something learned/remembered in church, at a friend’s house, in school, by a stranger, by a famous person.
  • Talk to your family and share a life lesson you’ve come to understand; or one you are struggling to get. (It’s OK to be vulnerable)
  • Talk about a favorite song to share and who, what or where it reminds you of.
  • Send a loving message to someone specific.

There are many more subjects you can pick from life’s journey to leave for relatives to enjoy and remember you.
